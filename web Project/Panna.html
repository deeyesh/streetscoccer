Panna Knock-out

About

Panna or nutmeg (sometimes just "meg" or "tunnel" as well) is a technique used in association football, field hockey or basketball, in which a player rolls or throws the ball between an opponent's legs (feet). The aim of the game is to beat your opponent by scoring more goals or ultimately playing the ball through the opponent's legs, otherwise known as a PANNA. Entry is free




Panna one vs one (1 vs 1): Performed in round pitch of 6m diameter and games are of 3 minutes. Opponents in the field play one versus one. The ball is thrown by a referee and game can be won either on points (who scores more goals) or making a panna to the opponent player.

Panna two vs two plyers (2 vs 2): Oval pitch of 6m x 9m and games of 4 minutes. The ball is thrown by a referee. A correct panna in 2vs2 variation is to play the ball intentionally between the legs of your opponent and to control the ball again (get the ball back). This can be done by either of the team mates.


Tournament Objectives

The objectives is to pass the ball into your opponent's legs, if you succeeded in doing this 3 times you won and move up to the next round


Playing Area

Round pitch of 6m diameter
